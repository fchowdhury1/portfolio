The Olympics—in the Palm of Your Hand!

Adobe is powering the mobile app experience of the Olympic Games, in partnership with NBC Olympics. Download the apps today!

Jul 11, 2012

Adobe's medal-winning Olympics innovation includes:

  • Sports fans in the U.S. can watch every minute of the Summer Olympics—and much more—with two mobile apps developed by Adobe.
  • This project has influenced Adobe's vision for our integrated video solution (Project Primetime), and it's a coup: We displaced Microsoft Silverlight!
  • Join the fun and send in some photos of the wacky places you plan to watch the Olympics, now that you're not tethered to the TV!

Fencing at your fingertips, hockey in your hands, and the pentathlon in your palm…  This year, for the first time in Olympics history, sports fans in the United States can watch anything and everything to do with the Olympics on their mobile devices—thanks to Adobe tools and technologies and our partnership with NBC Olympics, a division of the NBC Sports Group.technologies and our partnership with NBC Olympics, a division of the NBC Sports Group.

"We think this will be the biggest video streaming event in the history of the internet, in terms of how much video is being streamed," said CEO Shantanu Narayen, "and Adobe technology is going to play a big role in that."

"NBC Olympics is confident that Adobe, a proven technology leader and longtime partner, will deliver the best mobile event application experiences America has ever seen," said Gary Zenkel, president, NBC Olympics.

Setting New Records

Adobe has developed two apps for mobile devices and tablets in the United States:

  • NBC Olympics Live Extra: Live broadcasts of more than 3,500 hours of content, including all 32 sports, every athletic competition and all 302 medal events
  • NBC Olympics: A broad variety of additional content, including short-form highlights, schedules, live results, columns, athlete profiles and a new "Primetime Companion" feature (a second-screen experience for NBC's nightly Olympic broadcasts)

The apps are interconnected, so one can be launched from within the other. They are available at no cost on iPhone, iPad and iPod Touch via Apple's App Store and on select Android handset and tablet devices within Google Play. (A pay TV subscription that includes MSNBC is required, however, for NBC Olympics Live Extra. See below for access information.)

"It's been great working with NBC Olympics to change the way live sporting events are experienced by consumers. NBC is now able to create, deliver, monetize and measure the Olympics across mobile devices—using tools and technologies that make up Adobe's video solutions," said David Wadhwani, senior vice president, Digital Media Business.

"We're working closely with NBC Olympics and others to ensure that viewers have a seamless viewing experience with our two easy-to-navigate apps."

With NBC Olympics Live Extra, consumers can view live video streams of every event—all competitions and the opening and closing ceremonies. Special features include the ability to:

  • Customize the app, based on your interests
  • Set reminders for your favorite events—and record, pause, rewind or watch later
  • Authenticate once only with Adobe Pass
  • Share with friends via social media integration (Facebook, Twitter and Google +)

NBC Olympics is your complete guide to everything you could ever want to know about the London 2012 Olympics. Use it to find:

  • News stories and interviews
  • A vast array of short-form video-on-demand (VOD)
  • Schedules, medal counts, etc.
  • Bios of athletes and countries
  • Information on Olympic sites
  • Social media integration (Facebook, Twitter, Google +)

All Adobe, All the Times

A wide range of Adobe tools and technologies have been used to create these first-ever Olympics experiences. These include:

Creation (Adobe Tools)

  • Creative Suite 6 Tools (Photoshop, Illustrator)
  • Flash Platform Tools (Flash Builder, Flex)
  • Adobe Acrobat

Delivery (Adobe AIR)

  • Provides consistent content and video playback experience
  • Enables NBC Sports to leverage existing code without having to develop the apps from scratch for each platform
  • Runs across iOS and Android devices. With AIR, developers can reach all major device types and platforms, including 500+ million smartphones and tablets today

Authentication (Adobe Pass)

  • Ensures that millions of users have mobile access to all live streams of the world's largest event
  • Validates that users are entitled to view online content based on pay TV subscription ("TV Everywhere")
  • Supported by all major U.S. operators

Monetization (Adobe Auditude)

  • Delivers ads directly within both apps
  • Integrates with other NBC ad systems

Measurement (Adobe SiteCatalyst)

  • Provides NBC Sports with detailed analysis of how users engage with content and ads in both apps
  • Enables NBC Sports to optimize app experience as needed

The Inside StoryNBC is a long-standing Adobe customer. The company uses a range of Creative Suite products across both NBC and Universal Studios, for example, and it is also a long-time Omniture customer.

Discussions for this Olympics project began in March 2011, when Anita Mires, director, Strategic Alliances for Media and Agencies; Tom Nelms, principal, Business Development; and Jens Loeffler, senior technical evangelist, met with NBC Sports.

"We knew they had a problem in terms of delivering across all devices—both smartphones and tablets as well as iOS and Android," Anita said. "We began talking about the benefits of Adobe AIR, and this really resonated with them: They had a big job to do in a very short amount of time."

This deal is significant for Adobe in ways far beyond the size and visibility of the Olympic Games. As an example, its development has influenced Adobe's overall vision for our integrated video solution (called Project Primetime). In addition, the deal represents an important coup: Adobe displaced Microsoft Silverlight.

“We knew they had a problem in terms of delivering across all devices—both smartphones and tablets as well as iOS and Android.”– Anita Mires, director

"Tom is an incredible business development manager and Jens is a fantastic evangelist," Anita said. "They together created a great relationship with NBC Sports—one we could leverage to open up these new conversations around using our technology to displace Microsoft."

The Adobe Team

Employees from across the company have played a part in the successful development of these applications and the business partnership.

"This literally would not have happened without Tom and Jens," Anita said. "They partnered to incubate and sell the concept to NBC Sports. The project has grown to a large inter-organizational effort, utilizing many incredibly talented and dedicated people across Adobe. It's a great example of One Adobe."

The other key leads include:
  • XD and Strategic and Engagements teams (architects, design leads): Josh Ulm, Daniel Wabyick, Ramin Hedayatpour and Ethan Allen
  • Development, Quality and Architecture leads: Heidi Williams, Paul Ellis and David Devisser
  • Program manager: Shelley Sumner
  • Adobe Pass: Todd Greenbaum, Joel Huff
  • Auditude: Venkat Jonnadula
  • Digital Marketing: Ehren Hozumi Jui Jupe, Marijka Engel
  • Executive support/sponsorship: David Wadhwani and Michael Gough

Members of the Adobe team also collaborated with Apple on the video infrastructure apps. The overall experience was extremely positive, with Roger Pantos from Apple commenting on what a "great experience it has been to work together and we need to find more opportunities like this."

Bringing Home the Gold This historic partnership is a strong example of Adobe's innovation and industry leadership.

"This project showcases the power and breadth of Adobe's products to deliver beautiful and compelling experiences across mobile devices," Anita said.

There's no doubt about it: Adobe is bringing home the gold for powering an unbeatable viewer experience at the London 2012 Olympic Summer Games.
